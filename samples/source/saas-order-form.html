Software-as-a-Service Order Form

Between Brightpath Software, Inc. and Cascade Retail Group, LLC

This Order Form (the “Order”) is entered into as of March 1, 2026 (the “Effective Date”) between Brightpath Software, Inc., a Delaware corporation (the “Provider”), and Cascade Retail Group, LLC, a California limited liability company (the “Customer”), and is governed by the Provider’s Master Subscription Agreement incorporated herein by reference.

1. Subscription Services

The Provider shall make available to the Customer the Brightpath Analytics Platform — Business plan, provisioned for up to fifty (50) named user seats, together with standard email support and a 99.9% uptime service level.

2. Subscription Term

The initial subscription term is twelve (12) months from the Effective Date. Thereafter the subscription automatically renews for successive twelve (12) month terms unless either Party gives written notice of non-renewal at least thirty (30) days before the end of the then-current term.

3. Fees and Payment

The Customer shall pay an annual subscription fee of forty-eight thousand U.S. dollars (USD $48,000), invoiced annually in advance and due net thirty (30) days from the invoice date. Fees are non-refundable except as expressly provided in the Master Subscription Agreement.

4. Intellectual Property

As between the Parties, the Provider retains all right, title, and interest in and to the platform and all related intellectual property. The Provider grants the Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to access and use the platform during the subscription term solely for the Customer’s internal business purposes.

5. Termination

Either Party may terminate this Order for material breach that remains uncured thirty (30) days after written notice. Upon termination, the Customer’s access to the platform ceases and the Provider will make the Customer’s data available for export for thirty (30) days.

6. Limitation of Liability

Neither Party shall be liable for any indirect, incidental, or consequential damages. Each Party’s aggregate liability arising out of or related to this Order shall not exceed the total fees paid by the Customer in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim.

7. Governing Law

This Order shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of California, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.
